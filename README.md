## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyses employee attrition data for 500 employees 
across 7 departments and 7 Indian cities. The goal is to find 
why employees are leaving and give business recommendations 
to reduce attrition.

## 📈 Key Findings
- **30.2%** overall attrition rate — 151 out of 500 employees left
- **HR (36.2%)** and **Sales (35.3%)** are the highest-risk departments
- Employees doing **overtime** leave at **52.5%** vs **15.6%** without overtime
- **Below ₹30K salary** group has **83.3%** attrition — most critical finding
- **Age group 18–25** has the highest attrition at **47.4%**

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
1. Increase salaries for employees below ₹30,000
2. Limit overtime or provide overtime bonuses
3. Create mentorship programs for young employees (18–35)
4. Conduct quarterly job satisfaction surveys
